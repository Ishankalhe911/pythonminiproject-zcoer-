## 2. Practical Research Needs (Pages 7‚Äì8)

### Step 1: Sample Datasets for ML in Cybersecurity
- **Objective:** Provide realistic and standardized datasets to test and improve ML models.  
- **Importance:** Accurate, diverse, and labeled datasets are essential for evaluating performance.  
- **Actionable Focus:** Explore, clean, and summarize datasets; understand preprocessing and feature selection.

### Step 2: AI/ML-Powered Penetration Testing Mechanisms
- **Objective:** Develop intelligent systems to automate security testing and vulnerability assessment.  
- **Importance:** Speeds up detection of anomalies and potential breaches compared to manual methods.  
- **Actionable Focus:** Start with simulation of anomaly detection using simple ML methods.

### Step 3: Standardization of Frameworks and Protocols
- **Objective:** Establish common frameworks, metrics, and protocols for AI applications in cybersecurity.  
- **Importance:** Ensures reproducibility, interoperability, and secure deployment.  
- **Actionable Focus:** Learn standard data formats, evaluation methods, and reporting mechanisms.

### Step 4: Awareness Among Technologists and Developers
- **Objective:** Educate developers and technologists about AI-related security risks and mitigation strategies.  
- **Importance:** Awareness prevents misconfigurations, model misuse, and vulnerabilities.  
- **Actionable Focus:** Document failure cases, vulnerabilities, and safe practices as educational references.

### Step 5: Observatory / Research for Emerging Threats
- **Objective:** Continuously monitor, analyze, and predict new cybersecurity threats using AI.  
- **Importance:** Proactive monitoring is essential due to rapidly evolving threats.  
- **Actionable Focus:** Prototype dashboards, pipelines, or alert systems that aggregate threat data and identify trends.
